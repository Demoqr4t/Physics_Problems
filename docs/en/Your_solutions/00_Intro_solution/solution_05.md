To find the horizontal ($A_x$) and vertical ($A_y$) components of a vector, we use the trigonometric functions **cosine** and **sine**.

### 1. Identify the Formulas

When a vector $\vec{A}$ makes an angle $\theta$ with the horizontal axis:

* **Horizontal component ($A_x$):** $A_x = |\vec{A}| \cos(\theta)$
* **Vertical component ($A_y$):** $A_y = |\vec{A}) \sin(\theta)$

---

### 2. Calculate the Horizontal Component ($A_x$)

Substitute the magnitude ($15$) and the angle ($60^\circ$):


$$A_x = 15 \cdot \cos(60^\circ)$$

Since $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$ (or $1/2$):


$$A_x = 15 \cdot 0.5 = 7.5$$

### 3. Calculate the Vertical Component ($A_y$)

Substitute the magnitude and the angle:


$$A_y = 15 \cdot \sin(60^\circ)$$

Since $\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$:


$$A_y = 15 \cdot 0.866$$

$$A_y \approx 12.99$$

---

### Final Answer:

The components of the vector are:

* **Horizontal component ($A_x$):** **$7.5$**
* **Vertical component ($A_y$):** **$12.99$** (or $7.5\sqrt{3}$)
