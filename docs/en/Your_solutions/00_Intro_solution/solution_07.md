This is a classic puzzle that can be solved in two ways: the "hard way" (using an infinite series) and the "clever way" (using the total time elapsed). Since the fly and the bicycle are moving at constant speeds, the clever way is much faster.

### 1. Calculate the Total Time

The fly will stop flying only when the bicycle hits the wall. We need to find out how long that takes.

* **Distance to wall:** $10\text{ m}$
* **Bicycle speed:** $1\text{ m/s}$

Using the formula $t = \frac{d}{v}$:


$$t = \frac{10\text{ m}}{1\text{ m/s}} = 10\text{ seconds}$$

The bicycle (and the fly) will be traveling for exactly **10 seconds** before the collision.

---

### 2. Calculate the Fly's Distance

During those 10 seconds, the fly is moving at a constant speed of $2\text{ m/s}$. It doesn't matter how many times it turns around or how complex its path is; its speed remains constant.

Using the formula $d = v \cdot t$:

* **Fly speed:** $2\text{ m/s}$
* **Time:** $10\text{ s}$

$$d = 2\text{ m/s} \cdot 10\text{ s} = 20\text{ meters}$$

---

### Final Answer:

The fly travels a total distance of **20 meters** before it is crushed.
