To find the area under the curve of $f(x) = \sin(x)$ from $x=0$ to $x=\pi$, we use a **definite integral**.

### 1. Set up the Integral

The area $A$ is given by the integral of the function over the specified interval:


$$A = \int_{0}^{\pi} \sin(x) \, dx$$

### 2. Find the Antiderivative

The antiderivative of $\sin(x)$ is $-\cos(x)$. We apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus:


$$A = [-\cos(x)]_{0}^{\pi}$$

### 3. Evaluate at the Limits

Now, substitute the upper limit ($\pi$) and the lower limit ($0$) into the expression:


$$A = (-\cos(\pi)) - (-\cos(0))$$

Recall the values of the cosine function:

* $\cos(\pi) = -1$
* $\cos(0) = 1$

### 4. Calculate the Final Value

$$A = (-(-1)) - (-(1))$$

$$A = 1 - (-1)$$

$$A = 1 + 1$$

$$A = 2$$

### Final Answer:

The total area under the curve of $f(x) = \sin(x)$ from $0$ to $\pi$ is exactly **$2$** square units.
