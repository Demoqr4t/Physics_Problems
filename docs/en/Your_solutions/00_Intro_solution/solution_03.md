# Proportionality in Universal Gravitation

## The Formula
The gravitational force is given by:

$$
F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

## Given Changes
- Both masses are halved: $m \to \frac{1}{2}m$
- The distance is doubled: $r \to 2r$

## Step-by-Step Calculation

**1. Plug the changes into the formula**

$$
F_{new} = G \frac{(\frac{1}{2}m_1)(\frac{1}{2}m_2)}{(2r)^2}
$$

**2. Simplify the terms**

$$
F_{new} = G \frac{\frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2}{4 r^2}
$$

**3. Extract the numerical factor**

$$
F_{new} = \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{4} \cdot \left( G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right)
$$

**4. Final Relation**

$$
F_{new} = \frac{1}{16} F_{old}
$$

## Conclusion
If the distance is doubled and both masses are halved, the gravitational force changes by a factor of **1/16**.
