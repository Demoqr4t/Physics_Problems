To find the factor by which the gravitational force changes, we can compare the initial force ($F_{initial}$) to the new force ($F_{new}$) after the variables are modified.

### 1. The Initial State

The standard formula for the Universal Law of Gravitation is:


$$F_{initial} = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

### 2. The New Conditions

According to the problem, we are making the following changes:

* **Masses are halved:** $m_{1(new)} = \frac{1}{2}m_1$ and $m_{2(new)} = \frac{1}{2}m_2$
* **Distance is doubled:** $r_{new} = 2r$

### 3. Substitute into the Formula

Now, we plug these new values into the original equation:


$$F_{new} = G \frac{(\frac{1}{2}m_1) (\frac{1}{2}m_2)}{(2r)^2}$$

### 4. Simplify the Expression

First, simplify the numerator:


$$\frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4}$$

Next, simplify the denominator:


$$(2r)^2 = 4r^2$$

Now combine them:


$$F_{new} = G \frac{\frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2}{4r^2}$$

### 5. Determine the Scale Factor

To find the final factor, we pull the constants out to the front:


$$F_{new} = \left( \frac{\frac{1}{4}}{4} \right) \cdot G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

$$F_{new} = \left( \frac{1}{16} \right) \cdot F_{initial}$$

---

### Final Result

The gravitational force $F$ decreases by a factor of **$16$**. In other words, the new force is **$1/16$** of the original force.
